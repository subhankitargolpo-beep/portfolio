# Product Management Case Study Guidelines

This document outlines the standard for creating high-impact, deeply-detailed strategic case studies for the portfolio.

## 1. Structure & Narrative Flow (The Editorial Framework)
Every case study must follow a long-form editorial narrative that prioritizes **paragraphs over bullet points**. While bullet points are useful for lists, the core logic must be explained in flowing text to demonstrate strategic depth.

### A. The Problem Statement (The "Contextual Deep-Dive")
*   **The Hook:** Start with a high-stakes moment or an industry-shifting event.
*   **Detailed Challenge:** Instead of listing issues, write a cohesive narrative about the business situation. Explain the psychological state of the user and the financial pressure on the company.
*   **Strategic Risk:** Elaborate on the "what-if" scenario. Explain exactly why the company's existing model was becoming obsolete.

### B. Research & Discovery (The "Evidence-Based Narrative")
*   **Methodology:** Describe the investigative process in detail. How were users selected? What specific questions were asked during competitive teardowns?
*   **Logic-Driven Insights:** Write a paragraph for each key insight. Explain not just *what* was found, but *how* it fundamentally shifted your product intuition.
*   **Competitive Landscape:** Supplement tables with text that analyzes the "moat" or "vulnerability" of each competitor.

### C. Solution Exploration (The "Product Thinking Architecture")
*   **Pillar Logic:** For every initiative, write a dedicated paragraph explaining the **Mechanics** and **User Psychology**. 
    *   *Example:* Don't just say "we added tablets." Explain how the tablet's UI was designed to reveal hidden customer preferences, allowing for a consultation-style interaction that shifted the power dynamic in the retail aisle.
*   **The "How it Works":** A comprehensive breakdown of the technical and operational flow.
*   **Prioritization Narrative:** Explain the trade-offs. Why was "Feature A" discarded for "Feature B"? Discuss the RICE scores in the context of the business's current burn rate or growth goals.

### D. Interactive Dashboard (The "Evidence")
*   Every case study must include a visual dashboard showing:
    *   Pre-intervention vs. Post-intervention metrics.
    *   Segment-wise growth.
    *   A "North Star" metric chart.

### E. Learnings & v2 Vision
*   **The "Bad" News:** Honestly discuss a trade-off or a mistake.
*   **v2 Roadmap:** What would the product look like in its next major evolution?

---

## 2. Visual Standards
*   **Typography:** Use justified text for body paragraphs.
*   **Zebra Tables:** Use zebra-striped tables for all data comparisons.
*   **Callouts:** Use blockquotes for key quotes or "Product Manager Perspectives."

## 3. Data Integrity
*   Always cite sources (Nielsen, Deloitte, HBR, etc.).
*   If data is extrapolated, state the assumptions clearly.
