### Executive Summary

In early 2024, our team identified a critical drop-off in student engagement after the second week of our advanced programming course. As the Lead Product Strategist, I was tasked with diagnosing the issue and implementing a data-driven solution.

### The Challenge

User research indicated that students felt "isolated" and "unsupported" when working through complex coding modules. The existing static feedback system was too slow, often providing correction only after a student had already lost motivation.

### The Strategy

I proposed a three-pillar strategic intervention:
1. **Real-time AI Scaffolding**: Shifting from reactive grading to proactive "hints" generated by an LLM fine-tuned on pedagogical principles.
2. **Behavioral Gamification**: Implementing a non-linear progress tracking system that rewarded "resilience" (retrying difficult problems) rather than just correct answers.
3. **Peer Learning Circles**: Automating the creation of micro-communities based on shared learning pace.

### Execution & Results

The pilot program was rolled out to 1,200 students. 

- **Quantitative Impact**: We saw a direct correlation between AI hint engagement and course completion rates.
- **Qualitative Impact**: Student interviews revealed a significantly lower "perceived difficulty" score, even though the curriculum content remained unchanged.

### Key Learnings

This project reinforced my belief that in EdTech, **AI should be a companion, not just a grader**. The strategic shift from evaluation to support was the single biggest driver of success.
