### Executive Summary
Thenga Coco, a Kerala-based social enterprise, successfully repurposed over 140,000 discarded coconut shells into sustainable lifestyle products. However, as the brand scaled to meet international B2B demand from giants like Reliance and Microsoft, it hit a "growth ceiling" caused by natural product inconsistency. This case study details my product-led approach to engineering global-grade reliability into a handcrafted, natural supply chain.

---

### 1. The Problem Statement: The Growth Ceiling
As the Product Manager, I defined the core challenge as a tension between **artisan uniqueness** and **enterprise reliability**. 

**The Core Question:**
> “How might we deliver globally consistent, durable coconut-shell products at scale, while preserving Thenga’s core value proposition of natural variation and women-led sustainability?”

**Concrete Pain Points:**
*   **Size & Shape Variation:** Procuring 500+ identical shells is physically impossible due to natural growth, leading to "quality complaints" from bulk B2B buyers who expected uniformity.
*   **The Durability Risk:** Shells overexposed to the sun are prone to cracking, but identifying these in a raw "rescue" batch is complex, leading to expensive international refunds.
*   **Financial Leakage:** While domestic complaints are solved via quick replacements, international failures require full refunds due to logistics costs, directly impacting the bottom line.

**Market Context:** With the Indian sustainable lifestyle market projected to reach **USD 9.81 billion by 2025** (CAGR of 9.81%), the stakes were high. Thenga needed to transition from a "niche artisan brand" to a "global benchmark for circular products."

### 2. Research & Discovery: Validating the Solution Space
To validate the right approach, I structured discovery across four key streams:

| Research Stream | Method | Key Insight |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Internal Ops** | Value-stream mapping (Shell Rescue → Artisan Crafting → Packing) | Size-mismatch perception is amplified in bulk orders when recipients compare items side-by-side. |
| **Customer Segments** | 15+ B2B Interviews & D2C Surveys | International B2B clients have near-zero tolerance for defects; D2C customers actually value "unique stories." |
| **Artisan Workshops** | Shadowing women artisans in Thrissur & Wayanad | Standardization is possible in the "Sanding/Buffing" phase without harming the "Handcrafted" soul. |
| **Competitive Benchmarking** | Analyzing Bamboo & Ceramic alternatives | Successful brands solve the craft/scale tension through **Product Tiering**. |

---

### 3. Solution Strategy & Prioritization
Instead of a monolithic change, I implemented a **Portfolio Strategy**, splitting the product line to satisfy different user "Jobs to be Done."

#### 3.1 Solution Pillars
1.  **Product Architecture:** Differentiating between "Thenga Originals" (Artisan Unique) and "Thenga Standard" (Global Uniformity).
2.  **Shell Grading System:** Introducing a 3-tier grading process (A/B/C) based on diameter, thickness, and moisture indicators.
3.  **Quality Gates:** Adding two new QC checkpoints: Post-buffing (structural) and Pre-packaging (visual uniformity).
4.  **B2B Configurator:** A digital interface for enterprise clients to select size bands and volume tolerances.

#### 3.2 RICE Prioritization

### 4. Solution Design: Artifacts & User Flows

#### **The User Personas**
*   **Ananya (Conscious D2C):** Wants homeware that tells a story. Job: "Help me buy something unique."
*   **Rajesh (Corporate Procurement):** Needs 1,000 gifts for a Microsoft event. Job: "Help me look good with zero escalations."

#### **User Stories (Thenga Standard Line)**
*   *As a B2B buyer, I want to receive a pre-order sample kit so that I can validate uniformity before committing to a 5,000-unit order.*
*   *As a Quality Manager, I want to apply a simple grading template to raw shells so I can reject sun-damaged units early.*

#### **User Flow: The B2B Journey**
1.  **Selection:** Buyer selects "Thenga Standard" line for Corporate Gifting.
2.  **Spec Definition:** Buyer selects Volume (500ml ± 10%) and Finish.
3.  **Validation:** Buyer receives "Standard Sample Set."
4.  **Fulfillment:** Order routed to "Grade A" shell inventory with enhanced QC.

### 5. Impact & Metrics (Target Outcomes)
By segmenting the product line, we align operational costs with customer expectations:

*   **North Star Metric:** *Repeat B2B Order Rate* (Target: 40% increase YoY).
*   **Operational KPI:** *International Refund Rate* (Target: <2% of total export revenue).
*   **Sustainability KPI:** *Shells Rescued* (Target: 250,000+ shells by EOY).
*   **Social Impact:** *Artisan Earnings Uplift* (Target: 15% increase in monthly wages for rural women).

---

### 6. Learnings & Reflection
**The Crucial Trade-off:** 
Standardization for the B2B line requires artisans to work within tighter spec bands. This adds operational complexity but is the only way to protect the brand's reputation in international markets.

**Reflection:** 
The biggest takeaway is that **natural variance is a feature, not a bug—until it impacts functionality.** By creating a "Standard" line, we didn't lose the brand's soul; we gave it the structural integrity needed to survive on the global stage.
